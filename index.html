What is Photogrammetry and Topography?

Photogrammetry is a technique that uses photographs to obtain precise measurements and create 2D or 3D models of objects, while topography is the study of the Earth's surface, its characteristics and the representation of its terrain on maps and plans.

My Workflow

  1. Flight Consultation and Planning:I analyze your needs and terrain characteristics to design accurate and efficient data capture. I plan flight routes and topographic control points to ensure the necessary coverage and accuracy.
  2. Capture of Images and Topographic Data: I use drones equipped with high-resolution cameras and GNSS equipment to obtain centimeter-precise aerial imagery and topographic data. This process ensures a solid foundation for subsequent modeling and analysis.
  3. 3D Processing and Modeling: I align the images, generate dense point clouds, and generate digital terrain models. All the material is processed with specialized software to achieve precise georeferencing and high-fidelity topographic results.
  4. Delivery of Personalized Results:I present final products tailored to your requirements: orthophotos, interactive 3D models, contour lines, contour maps, and topographic plans ready for integration into engineering or construction projects.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is photogrammetry and topography?

The accuracy of surveying and photogrammetry varies depending on the methods and equipment used. Traditional surveying, especially with modern total stations, can achieve accuracy from millimeters to centimeters, making it ideal for detailed engineering and construction work. On the other hand, photogrammetry, which uses aerial imagery taken by drones or aircraft, typically has lower accuracy, typically within several centimeters, although this accuracy improves significantly when ground control points are used.

How long does it take to complete a project?

The time to complete a surveying or photogrammetry project depends on the size of the area, the required accuracy, and available resources. In traditional surveying, a small survey can take from a few hours to several days, while large projects can last weeks. Photogrammetry, especially with drones, is typically faster for large areas, as it can capture large surfaces in just a few hours, although image processing can take additional days.
